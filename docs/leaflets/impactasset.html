Where the Habit Is Won or Lost

University is where the gym habit often start, yet it is also where it frequently dies. Students arrive highly motivated, often signing up with friends, but the critical first month decides everything. Without structured accountability, the initial excitement of a new routine quickly fades. Students struggle to transition from relying on willpower to establishing a cemented habit. Attendance quietly drops, friend groups flake on each other, and students are left frustrated and £480 out of pocket on wasted memberships. GymJam was designed to intervene in this exact window of failure. Built for the friend groups students already have, the app engineers accountability when intrinsic motivation runs out. By making individual pledges visible to the entire group, GymJam creates a shared contract. The Group Pot introduces small, loss aversion stakes, meaning skipping a session isn't just letting yourself down, it’s letting your friends down and losing your own money. We carry established friend groups through that fragile early period where routines are typically abandoned. The result is a profound ripple effect: GymJam turns motivated but flaky students into active individuals, fostering real world campus communities and laying down preventative health habits that pay dividends long after graduation.
